AI in the workplace is a fundamental component in assisting with workload, it is arguably a more efficient companion to help with daily tasks and as a guide for others.

However, over-reliance on AI, specifically in the workplace can lead to increased operational and security risks, even errors or biased responses which would be detrimental to the company or operation taking place.

Key Issues

The causes we champion

◆
AI-Driven Job Displacement

Accelerated AI adoption — especially in manufacturing, logistics, and back-office functions — threatens to replace entire workforces. We push for transition support, retraining programs, and labor protections.

→
◆
Over-reliance & Critical Thinking

Excessive dependence on AI erodes employees' critical thinking, creativity, and professional judgment. Without guardrails, workplaces risk producing a workforce that defers to algorithms over human insight.

→
◆
Security & Bias Risks

Integrating AI into company operations opens new vectors for cyberattacks and data breaches. Biased training data can also lead to discriminatory decisions in hiring, performance reviews, and resource allocation.

→

"AI's annual growth rate is expected to soar by 54%, with around 80% of employers already harnessing its power — yet the greatest risk is not the technology itself, but the absence of human judgment alongside it."

— New Horizons, AI in the Workplace Report

Get Involved

There are many ways
to take action

01
Implement Human-in-the-Loop (HITL)

Advocate for workplaces to ensure AI systems are never fully autonomous. Human workers must supervise and review AI-generated outputs to prevent errors, bias, and misinformation — and to uphold ethical standards and digital laws.

Source →
02
Strengthen Government AI Oversight

Push for legal frameworks that monitor and regulate AI development. Support the proposed National AI Commission (NAIC) under the Department of Science and Technology to ensure AI protects workers, sensitive data, and national interests.

Source →
03
Invest in Workforce Retraining

Support programs that help displaced workers — especially in manufacturing and logistics — adapt to an AI-augmented economy. Retraining initiatives ensure that efficiency gains don't come at the cost of livelihoods.

Source →
04
Demand Transparent AI Practices

Companies integrating AI into HR, operations, and decision-making must be transparent about how those systems work. Advocate for algorithmic audits, bias testing, and clear accountability chains within organizations.

Source →
